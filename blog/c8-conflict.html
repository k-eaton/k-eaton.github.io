Zen and the Art of Terror Management

  • January 28th, 2015
  • Cultural

This week's blog assignment is about problem resolution in the "workplace". Identify a conflict you've had and talk about how you've resolved it.

Sorry, but I'm almost 40 coming in from a prior career. I know how to handle conflict in the workplace. Passive aggression works wonders, as does making art work that exemplifies your feelings about the subject and the focus of your rage.

Let's talk about something more relevant: fear. This bootcamp is beyond anything I've ever done/experienced/thought of. Going in I'm concerned about keeping up, not whether or not I can keep it together and not take my frustration out on my classmates. I don't know what the magic formula is. And that's the hard part, not letting the fear become this all consuming thing that takes me down, crash and burn.

I think the trick is going to be focus, and that's something I struggle with if I'm not engaged. Fortunately I'm interested in the topics presented, but it's still a monumental task to contemplate. I'm scared pure and simple. The hours are insane, my lack of access to friends limits my access to people who help me understand topics I get stuck on, and I'm a social creature in general. It's going to be such a big change.

But I want this. A lot. And hopefully that will be enough.
